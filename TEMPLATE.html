CLIMATE CHANGE

Climate change is defined as a long-term shift in global or regional climate patterns, however, when occurring naturally this is a slow process that has taken place over hundreds and thousands of years. The influence of human activity on this natural cause has led to a much faster rate, and it's only increasing. One main aspect of climate change is global warming, the long-term heating of the Earth’s climate system. The main contributor to this would be GreenHouse Gas (GHG) emissions. Carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases act like a blanket, absorbing IR radiation and preventing it from escaping into outer space. Over the last century, the burning of fossil fuels, including gasoline, has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2), accounting for about two-thirds of GHGs. Cars and trucks collectively account for nearly one-fifth of all US emissions, emitting 24 pounds of carbon dioxide and other global-warming gases for every gallon of gas.1 Climate change cannot be solved all together, however there are ways to mitigate the amount of emissions. The solution is electric powered vehicles, such as the Tesla model 3.
